What do countries where most forcibly displaced people come from have in common?

Apart from war and conflicts, economic hardships and social restrictions force millions to flee their homes.

By Stephanie Lacouture Ospina | Dec. 11, 2018

For the last five years, the world has been living the worst humanitarian crises since World War II. From small countries in Asia, through different regions in Africa, to once rich, peaceful, and prosperous countries like Venezuela more and more people are living their places or origins. Citing UNHCR Global Trends 2017 report “It means the world’s forcibly displaced population has now overtaken that of the United Kingdom and reached another record high”. But what is it that force them to leave their home and loves ones, besides the wars and conflicts going on in some of these countries?

Where are the largest forcibly displaced populations from?

Source: United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR)

Without a doubt, Syrians are in the center of the biggest humanitarian and human rights crises taking place recently. About 13 million people, including men, women, and children, have fled their country’s civil war. Although officially no longer in crisis, Colombians are the second largest population forcibly displaced, with almost eight million people having flee their country in the last decade due to terror acts caused by the guerrillas. The Congolese population come in the unfortunate third place. More than five million people fled the Democratic Republic of Congo after a decades long conflict. The same number applies for the Afghans that have been forcibly displaced from their home fearing the Taliban regime and the recent wars that haven taken place in the country.

Per reported by UNHCR “other large displaced populations included people from South Sudan (4.4 million), Iraq (3.3 million), Somalia (3.2 million), Sudan (2.7 million), Yemen (2.1 million), Nigeria (2.0 million), and Ukraine (2.0 million)”. According to this information, most of the forcibly displaced populations are from East African and Middle Eastern regions.
